About Melonama

Melanoma is a type of skin cancer that originates in melanocytes, the cells responsible for producing melanin, the pigment that gives skin its color. It can develop anywhere on the skin, but it most commonly occurs on areas exposed to the sun, such as the face, neck, arms, and legs. Melanoma can also occur in areas that don't receive much sun exposure, including the palms of the hands, soles of the feet, and even under the nails.The common parts where the melanoma attacks is..

  • Face
  • Neck
  • Arms
  • Legs

Melanoma often begins as a new mole or an existing mole that undergoes changes in size, shape, color, or texture. It can also appear as a dark spot that looks different from the surrounding skin. While it's usually diagnosed in its early stages, when it's most treatable, melanoma can also spread to other parts of the body if not detected and treated early. Risk factors for melanoma include excessive sun exposure, a history of sunburns, having fair skin, having many moles or unusual moles, a family history of melanoma, and a weakened immune system. However, it's essential to note that anyone, regardless of skin color, can develop melanoma. Regular skin self-exams and annual skin screenings by a dermatologist are essential for detecting melanoma and other skin cancers early, when they are most treatable. Prevention strategies include avoiding excessive sun exposure, using sunscreen with a high SPF, wearing protective clothing, and seeking shade during peak sun hours
